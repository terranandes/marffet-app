## 2. Fact Sorting: Correctness vs. Incorrectness [CV] & [SPEC]

### ✅ Correctness (The Golden Rules)
1. **Unfiltered Universe**: We must correlate strictly against MoneyCome's `stock_list_s2006e2026_unfiltered.xlsx`. 
2. **Nominal Prices**: Prices MUST be absolute "Nominal" (Unadjusted) closing prices. The `ROICalculator` natively handles dividend reinvestment and split share-multiplication. Using "Adjusted" prices double-counts the reinvestment.
3. **Absolute Dividends**: Dividend yield (Cash & Stock) must be recorded exactly as declared by the company (Official TWSE or Goodinfo). YFinance's dividend fields are notoriously scaled by splits and cannot be trusted for native calculation.
4. **ISIN Initialization**: The master stock list must be populated from the official TWSE/TPEx ISIN endpoint to guarantee tracking of both active and historically delisted codes.
5. **Dividend Timing Rules**: MoneyCome strictly enforces the "去年留倉部位" (last year's remaining position) rule. Dividends are paid out on the shares held at the *end of the previous year*, before the current year's new capital is injected.
6. **Split Neutrality**: Stock splits (e.g., 0050's 4-for-1 ETF split) are value-neutral. They must be detected (price drops >40%) and applied as a multiplier to the holding count, not treated as a loss.

### ❌ Incorrectness (The Fallacies to Discard)
1. **Bankrupt Terminal Math (The -100% Fallacy)**: The recent `calculator.py` patch assumed that *any* stock that stops trading before 2026 (e.g., 1606 Kolin) should drop to a `$0` terminal value. **This is fundamentally wrong.** Many stocks that stop trading (e.g., 2325 SPIL, 2456 Chilisin) were **Acquired mathematically at a premium**. By forcing `$0`, we crashed their CAGR to -100%, causing the massive Match Rate drop.
   - **Correction**: If a stock stops trading early, its capital is "frozen" and its terminal value is evaluated based on the *last available nominal closing price*.
2. **TPEx Native API for History**: The TPEx official API silently truncates historical queries to the current year (BUG-116).
   - **Correction**: Historical price boundaries must be queried via the YFinance batch loader with `auto_adjust=False`.
3. **YFinance Mass Fetch Reliability**: Relying on `yfinance.download(period="max")` is brittle. It suffers from connection drops, silent ticker exclusions, and stealth price adjustments (BUG-115).
   - **Correction**: The core daily price load must originate from our local `MI_INDEX` nominal backup scripts, using YFinance strictly as a fallback or patch tool.
